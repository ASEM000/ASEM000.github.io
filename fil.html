Overview
Investigation on dispersion mechanisms of pollutants dispersion under stratified layers mimicking ocean conditions using machine learning


Mahmoud Asem,Professor Hyoungsoo Kim Link

Abstract

Motivated by the recent occurrence of the Deepwater Horizon oil spill accident in the Gulf of Mexico, we plan to study the effect of stratification (density layer variation experienced in sea layers) on pollutants' behavior, e.g., oil and ethanol plumes.

The research aims to characterize and understand the effects of stratification in a quiescent environment and its relation to the oil break up and ethanol trapping in layers boundary. We believe that the current problem has been rarely investigated systematically, which is possibly crucial to estimate or predict how the pollutants, including oil and HNS (Hazard Noxious Substance), disperse in the ocean. My objective is that understanding jet behavior under the effects of stratification will provide some guidelines for the cleaning process. To investigate and characterize this problem, we perform the visualization experiments of the pollutant plumes in a two-layer density environment. For the first apparatus, we study the silicon oil jet behavior impinging toward the interface between two fluids having a different density where the jet is injected from the bottom to the light fluid environment. By varying the injection flow rate, the two layers' height, and density ratio, we discover that sharp density difference across the two layers interface triggers coiling/folding of silicon oil jet. The second apparatus is to study the effect of sharp density difference on an ethanol plume's trapping. For this experiment, we test the injection flow rate of ethanol, the top fluid's layer height, and the density of the top fluid.

The experiment analysis is assisted by state-of-the-art super-resolution generative adversarial network technique to improve low-resolution data images' quality. Based on this technique, we analyze the plume shape of the pollutant dispersion in the stratified layers. Moreover, we attempt to predict the jet's spatiotemporal behavior using deep learning-based CNN-LSTM Wiggly-net.
